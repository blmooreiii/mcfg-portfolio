Our Story

Rooted in the Lowcountry, Built for Beyond.

Our Purpose

Marble Ceilings For Good (MCFG) is a non-profit innovation lab intent on producing user-friendly apps that target individuals who are often the last to know about help-seeking technologies.

This delayed access makes them more likely to slip through the cracks and not receive the assistance they need so desperately.

Our Origins

This endeavor is a lifetime in the making. I was taught early that if I could improve the lives of others, then I should. MCFG is the culmination of the lifelong influences of three people: my father (Bertram Moore, Jr), my mother (Viola Moore) and my aunt (Linda Gadson).

My father advised me that my interest in cutting edge technology should not just be a way to make a living, but that I should use my acquired knowledge to do something impactful. It was ingrained in me that I had a responsibility to use technology as a tool for good.

On the other hand, my mother was my earliest example of “Love thy neighbor”. If someone was in need, she was on the front lines making sure they got what they needed. She worked alongside my aunt who was the Executive Director at Rural Mission Inc, a non-profit organization that provided for the many needs of Sea Island residents in South Carolina.

"Rural Mission planted the seeds.
Marble Ceilings For Good is what has sprouted."
The Work Continues

They worked endless hours to serve those who came to them for help. Whether they distributing food, paying past due utility bills or repairing dilapidated homes, people knew that they could count on Vi and Linda. It was their calling. Each time they sat with someone in their most difficult moments, I paid attention.

Together, that trio taught me that community service isn't an abstract concept that you list on your resume’. It involves showing up for your neighbors and taking action regardless of the temperature outside or time of day because need often does not have perfect timing. I realized that serving others may not always be convenient, but you could make getting help more convenient. Rural Mission closed in 2019, but what it stood for didn't die. It still lives on in seeing the humanity of all people and that people on the margins deserve much more than good intentions.

Our Vision

We are creating useful technologies for people who would typically fall through the cracks of systems traditionally designed to help them. Our products provide common sense solutions for people in unfortunately common situations. We innovate for the domestic violence victim trying to find a safe place, for the needy family in search of benefits for which they might qualify, and for the patient managing a chronic illness without the necessary support. Our technology meets people where they are when they need it.

MCFG is a trusted ecosystem of tools that promote safety, care, and support. Each product we build addresses at least one of those functions and together they address the needs of vulnerable communities whose crises often occur consecutively and/or simultaneously.

Bastet

Provides up-to-date information for domestic violence victims (hotlines, emergency shelters, and other relevant resources).

Seshat

Navigates through benefits systems for disadvantaged populations in South Carolina making the complex state and federal assistance programs more manageable.

Project Malcolm

Features a patient-facing portal for the Sickle Cell Anemia community to access important data about and guidance on managing the disease.

This is just the beginning. The ecosystem will expand as needs arise.

Our Future

Rural Mission didn't close because the work was finished. Marble Ceilings For Good is a renewed promise to the communities that shaped us, to the people our products serve, and to Bertram, Viola, and Linda, whose lives inspired it. We are carrying those valuable lessons learned on the Sea Islands into every product we ship, every partnership we build, every person we reach.

The baton is in our hands now.
We intend to run with it.
