001 — Why We Exist Manifesto
"Innovation shouldn't be a
zip code privilege."

Marble Ceilings For Good is a South Carolina nonprofit proving that the most important technology of this decade doesn't have to start in Silicon Valley. We build apps, platforms, and research tools that close the gap between communities and the systems meant to serve them.

No. i

Community-First Technology

Every product we build begins with listening — to survivors, advocates, and the people closest to the problem. Not to market research. Not to investors.

No. ii

Research & Advocacy

We translate community knowledge into data that moves policy, programs, and resources where they're needed most.

No. iii

Capacity Building

We share our tools, methods, and learnings with other organizations — because the mission is bigger than any one project.

Featured — File 01

Quiet protection, not crisis branding.

Named for the Egyptian goddess who guards women and homes, Bastet works in seconds, leaves no trace, and feels nothing like a traditional help app. On a home screen it reads as an indie game. Inside, it's a composed, private tool connecting survivors to 22 verified South Carolina shelters.

  • 01Trust is the product — every decision evaluated against whether it builds or breaks user trust
  • 02Privacy is table stakes — no account, no history, no stored location, no trace
  • 03Discretion over visibility — quick exit clears the app in under 120ms
  • 04Navigation tool only — no chat, no push notifications, no noise

004 — Our Origins Rural Mission → MCFG
A renewed promise

Rooted in the Lowcountry, built for beyond.

This endeavor is a lifetime in the making. I was taught early that if I could improve the lives of others, then I should.

MCFG is the culmination of the lifelong influences of three people: my father (Bertram Moore, Jr), my mother (Viola Moore) and my aunt (Linda Gadson). My father advised me that my interest in cutting-edge technology should not just be a way to make a living — that I should use my acquired knowledge to do something impactful.

My mother and aunt were the front lines of service. As Executive Director at Rural Mission Inc, my aunt provided for the many needs of Sea Island residents. Together, they taught me that serving others may not always be convenient — but you could make getting help more convenient.

"Rural Mission planted the seeds. Marble Ceilings For Good is what has sprouted."

Rural Mission closed in 2019, but what it stood for didn't die. It lives on in seeing the humanity of all people — and knowing that people on the margins deserve much more than good intentions. The baton is in our hands now. We intend to run with it.

006 — Leadership Board of Directors

The people who said yes first.

Our board brings together expertise in public health, community organizing, technology, and advocacy — with roots in the communities we serve. Credibility and community aren't opposites here. They're the same thing.

Founder / E.D.

Bertram L. Moore III

Founder & Executive Director

A technologist who builds for people the system overlooks. After nearly 20 years at a Fortune 50 company, he's turning his focus to communities that don't typically benefit from innovation. His approach is simple: start with "How can I help?" and work from there.

Board Chair

Dr. Bertrina L. Scott

Board Chair

Dr. Scott holds a Ph.D. in Developmental Psychology, specializing in child and adolescent development. As Board Chair, she provides governance oversight and strategic direction — ensuring MCFG's work remains grounded in rigorous practice and community trust. She was among the first to believe in this organization's potential.
